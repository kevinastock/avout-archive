First Among Equals

First appearance and context

The title is introduced during a meal-time conversation on the eve of Apert, when Fraa Corlandin is identified as the FAEâ€”First Among Equalsâ€”of the Order of the New Circle. The mention comes amid preparations to receive new members and to hold formal rites following the opening.

Definition and rationale

In-text, "FAE" is explicitly expanded as "First Among Equals." It denotes a leadership role within an order while emphasizing collegial parity. Usage in the same period also applies the title to a chapter: a theorical geologist is described as the FAE of the Edharian chapter. The narrative does not yet describe how the role is chosen, how long it is held, or what specific authorities or duties it entails.

Usage at Orithena

At Orithena, a Lineage (ancient scholarly order) identifies its public-facing leader as the FAE. Their FAE, Fraa Landasher, is named as one of the gate interlocutors and later offers a formal welcome and toast during a communal meal. This usage suggests a collegial head who represents the community in external-facing duties while preserving the emphasis on parity among peers.

Current status
