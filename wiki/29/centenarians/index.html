Hundreders

First Appearance and Context

At the Concent of Saunt Edhar, the Hundreders occupy the Centenarian Math adjacent to the Decenarian Math. In shared rites within the great hall, their processions and seating are associated with the south nave behind their screen.

Concept and Description

Hundreders (singular: "Hundreder") are the cohort whose portal opens on a hundred-year cycle. Their long cadence and relative seclusion shape how they are discussed inside the maths, and they are often contrasted with other cohorts by cycle length and outlook.

Use in Current Discourse

During a broad Evocation that led to a Convox, hierarchs paired Tenners with Hundreders for travel, since many Hundreders would need help speaking Fluccish and coping with the worldly realm while maintaining Discipline during Peregrin. Accounts from the organizing period note a range of reactions within the cohortâ€”for example, some Centenarian suurs declined to heed an Ita who addressed the assembly, while others engaged through their assigned Tenners. These observations describe individual moments and should not be taken as universal traits of the cohort.

In one Peregrin group, a companion is described as "the youngest of the Hundreders," illustrating that the cohort spans a range of ages and experiences within a given party. In the same vein, a narrator dismissively refers to certain rumors as "nutty Hundreder fads"â€”a stereotype recorded here as perception rather than fact.
