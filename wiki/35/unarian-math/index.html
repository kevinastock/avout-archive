Unarian Math

First Appearance and Context

During Apert, guided tours gather at the bridge to the Year Gate and cross into the heart of the Unarian Math. Near the approach stands a wedge-shaped slab of red granite listing fraas and suurs who fell in a one-hour sortie during the Third Sack; the tumulus is aimed inward toward the clock to emphasize that those commemorated returned. The Hylaean Way begins nearby and leads visitors through exhibit chambers.

  • Gate association: Associated with the Year Gate for annual movement and tours during the opening.

  • Part of the Concent of Saunt Edhar, sharing rites, mechanisms, and campus spaces with neighboring maths.

Relationships

  • Resident community for the Unarians.
  • Functional link to the Decenarian precinct via the connecting labyrinth and regular graduations during the opening.

Descriptions/Characteristics

  • Architecture and exhibits: The Hylaean Way‚Äôs entrance chamber is clad in greenish-brown shale rich with fossils; beyond lies a Rotunda lit by a triangular oculus, with celebrated statuary, followed by didactic galleries (including a Diorama Chamber) and an elliptical hall on the theoric golden age. Current Status/Location

Active within the concent, receiving visitors during Apert and preparing dormitories and grounds for the incoming one-year cohort as the opening draws to a close.

Summary:

Unarian Math is the one-year precinct of the Concent of Saunt Edhar, linked to the Year Gate and designed to receive visitors during Apert. It houses the Hylaean Way exhibits and a wedge-shaped memorial to those who returned during the Third Sack.
