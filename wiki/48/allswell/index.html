Allswell

Definition

Allswell is described as a naturally occurring chemical that, when present in sufficient concentration in the brain, engenders the feeling that everything is fine. A dictionary entry credits its isolation to Theors early in the A.R. period and says it was later made available as a pharmaceutical. It became ubiquitous when a common weed, later called Blithe, was engineered to produce it as a byproduct of its metabolism. Blithe is handled intramuros (inside the walls) under burn-on-sight procedures as one of The Eleven.
