Unarian Math

First Appearance and Context

Named alongside the Decenarian and Centenarian communities when the concentâ€™s river-bank bastions opened their gates during Apert. The same waterworks that charge the gate mechanisms also form part of the physical barrier between the Unarian and the Decenarian Math.

Roles/Actions and Affiliations

  • Initial community for new Avout: many spend at least a year under its Discipline before moving on to a decennial cohort in the Decenarian community.
  • Rare early advancement: exceptional One-off cases may pass through a connecting labyrinth directly to the Decenarian side.
  • Gate association: linked to the Year Gate, which opens on an annual cycle and is also called the Unarian Gate.
  • Part of the Concent of Saunt Edhar and participates in the shared rites and openings observed there.

Relationships

  • Order/identity: resident community for the Unarians.
  • Counterpart community: closely tied to the Decenarian side by both the labyrinth connection and recurring graduations at Apert.

Descriptions/Characteristics

  • Boundaries and works: ponds, canals, and fountains fed from the upstream aqueduct form part of the barrier between Unarian and Decenarian areas within the concent.
  • Gates and mechanism: one of the outer-wall bastions houses the gate for this community; it is actuated by water-driven machinery synchronized to the great clock.

Current Status/Location

Active within the concent during the current Decennial Apert, with members expected to advance into the Decenarian cohort as usual. Specific leadership, layout, and census have not been described.

Summary:

Unarian Math is a mathic community associated with the Unarians and the one-year gate. It often serves as an initial community for new avout, with rare One-off cases advancing early via a labyrinth to the Decenarian Math.
