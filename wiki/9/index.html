Chapter summary

After a troubling dream of clock errors and wrong bell changes, the narrator rises for Apert on the first morning of the new year. The community gathers in the Mynster for a music-led rite, and at sunrise the mechanisms activate as the Day, Year, and Decade gates open. The smooth opening and shared emotion ease the unease implied by the dream.

  • A Dictionary entry outlines the Ita through different ages, presenting them as a later-era artisanal caste tasked with operating syntactic-device subsystems within concents.
  • The narrator dreams of orrery and bell errors and suspects the Ita are involved, setting a tone of unease before the day begins.
  • On the first morning of the new year, Apert begins; the fraas don formal wraps with bolts and chords, improvise sandals, and mark themselves as Tenners with glowing spheres.
  • The community assembles in the Mynster for a music-only processional synchronized to the clock, awaiting the moment of sunrise.
  • At sunrise, light from the Starhenge prism washes the chancel, the central weight drops, and the Day Gate opens.
  • Auxiliary weights shaped as a cube and an octahedron descend to open the Year Gate and the Decade Gate; applause and even reactions behind the Thousanders' screen mark the moment.
