Warden Fendant

The Warden Fendant is the outward-watching office of a math, responsible for maintaining sentinels and observation along the rooftop perimeter of the Mynster.

First Appearance and Context

The office is situated in an aerie constructed on the flat roof above the Mynster's vaulted ceilings. Around the central tower, the Praesidium, an open periphery walkway allows sentinels to pace a full circuit, with supporting braces below and twin themes of outward- and inward-facing oversight embodied in how the upper works are arranged.

Roles and Responsibilities

  • Maintain an outward watch over the surrounding lands from the aerie and rooftop circuit.
  • Field and direct sentinels who patrol the perimeter.
  • Coordinate with the inward-facing authority of the Warden Regulant when situations require joint action.
  • Review external movements and risks and advise the math's leadership accordingly.
  • Clear and secure upper levels ahead of solemn services and large gatherings.
  • Control access to the upper works, including sealing the portcullis to the court and restricting the buttress stair when needed.
