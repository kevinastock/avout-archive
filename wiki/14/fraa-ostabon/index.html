Fraa Ostabon

Fraa Ostabon is an avout seen among the fids at the Concent of Saunt Edhar. In a tightly packed discussion in Saunt Grod's chalk hall, led by Grandsuur Tamura, he is called on (as "Fid Ostabon") and gives the class's clearest description of the Temnestrian Iconography.

First Appearance and Context

Ostabon appears during a lesson on the recurring "iconographies" that SÃ¦culars use to imagine the mathic world. With the full crop of fids gathered, he answers Tamura's question about the Temnestrian Iconography, describing it as a two-phase caricature: first portraying avout as comical oddities, then revealing a darker second phase in which contact with avout is blamed for seducing the young, unhinging a responsible parent, or leading a public figure into folly. The session concludes with permission for the crop to go extramuros during Apert.

Role and Actions

  • Provides the succinct explanation of the Temnestrian Iconography during the class review.
  • Participates as part of the assembled fids preparing for the freedoms of Apert.

Affiliations and Relationships

  • Member of the fids' cohort at the Concent of Saunt Edhar.
  • Present in the same cohort observed by Fraa Erasmas and peers such as Suur Tulia, under the instruction of Grandsuur Tamura.

Description

Ostabon is identified as a twenty-one-year-old fraa with a ragged beard. His manner during the discussion is a little brusque, but he regains the floor and completes his answer clearly.

Status

As of the cited discussion, he is within the concent and among those granted leave to go extramuros during Apert.
