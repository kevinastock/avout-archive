Fraa Corlandin

First Appearance and Context

Fraa Corlandin is shown sitting across from Fraa Erasmas in the Refectory on the eve of Apert at the Concent of Saunt Edhar. As FAE of the Order of the New Circle, he opens a courteous conversation that amounts to sounding out Erasmas as a prospective recruit ahead of Eliger.

Roles/Actions and Affiliations

  • First Among Equals of the Order of the New Circle, acting as a visible leader and engaging Tenners ahead of Eliger.
  • Has taught modern (post-Reconstitution) history; in that context he is remembered for the adage "There is no right way to build a clock," a gentle way of saying that Saunt Edhar's praxics could be a bit improvised.
  • In conversation he practices polite, indirect talk and remarks that he does not know enough about the stars even to "say something wrong."
  • He expresses regret that he has not been suvined by Fraa Orolo more, acknowledging Orolo's reputation as an impressive theorician.
  • During preparations leading into the closing of Apert, he is seen with other New Circle avout rehearsing a carol to be sung on Tenth Night.
  • Among some peers he is perceived as actively recruiting for the New Circle, a style that draws occasional skepticism.

Relationships

  • Fraa Erasmas: engaged courteously as a prospective New Circle recruit.
  • Fraa Orolo: referenced respectfully; Corlandin acknowledges Orolo's standing and work.
  • Suur Trestanas: previously in a liaison with Corlandin; after her appointment as Warden Regulant, rumor held that the liaison continued.

Descriptions/Characteristics

Polite and indirect in conversation; remembered for a wry teaching line: "There is no right way to build a clock." In group settings he presents as confident and at ease among fellow New Circle avout.

Current Status/Location

Active within the community at Saunt Edhar, serving as FAE of the Order of the New Circle and engaging prospective members as Eliger approaches. During the ongoing decennial Apert he participates in public-facing preparations, including a carol rehearsal for Tenth Night.

Summary:

An avout who serves as First Among Equals (FAE) of the Order of the New Circle at the Concent of Saunt Edhar, and who has taught modern history; he is remembered for saying there is "no right way to build a clock." He engages Tenners ahead of Eliger and is perceived by some peers as actively recruiting for the New Circle.
